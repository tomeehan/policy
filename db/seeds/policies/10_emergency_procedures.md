**POLICY STATEMENT**

Acme Care Services is committed to ensuring the safety of all service users, staff and visitors in emergency situations. This policy sets out our emergency procedures and the responsibilities of staff in responding to emergencies.

**SCOPE**

This policy covers:

- Fire emergencies
- Medical emergencies
- Security incidents
- Severe weather
- Utility failures
- Missing persons
- Other critical incidents

### Fire Emergencies

**On Discovering a Fire**

a) Raise the alarm immediately

b) Call 999

c) Evacuate the building using the nearest safe exit

d) Close doors behind you to prevent fire spread

e) Assemble at the designated assembly point

f) Do not use lifts

g) Do not re-enter the building until authorised

**Fire Prevention**

- Fire exits must be kept clear at all times
- Fire extinguishers must be checked regularly
- Fire drills will be conducted at least every six months
- All staff must attend fire safety training

### Medical Emergencies

In a medical emergency:

a) Call 999 immediately if life-threatening

b) Provide first aid within your competence

c) Do not move the person unless necessary for safety

d) Comfort and reassure the person

e) Record the incident and actions taken

### Missing Persons

If a service user is missing:

a) Conduct an immediate search of the premises

b) Notify the manager

c) Contact the police if the person is not found quickly

d) Notify next of kin

e) Complete an incident report

### Business Continuity

We maintain business continuity plans to ensure essential services can continue during emergencies. Key elements include:

- Emergency contact lists
- Alternative staffing arrangements
- Communication protocols
- Recovery procedures

### Incident Reporting

All emergencies and incidents must be:

- Reported to management immediately
- Documented on an incident report form
- Notified to relevant authorities where required (CQC, HSE, etc.)
- Reviewed to identify lessons learned

### Training

All staff will receive training on emergency procedures as part of their induction and regular refresher training.
