**POLICY STATEMENT**

Acme Care Services is committed to preventing the spread of infection and maintaining a safe environment for service users, staff and visitors. This policy outlines our infection prevention and control procedures.

**AIM OF THE POLICY**

To minimise the risk of infection by:

- Implementing effective hand hygiene practices
- Using personal protective equipment (PPE) appropriately
- Following safe working practices
- Managing outbreaks effectively
- Training all staff in infection control

### Standard Precautions

Standard precautions should be applied to all service users at all times:

**Hand Hygiene**

Hands must be washed or sanitised:

- Before and after contact with service users
- After removing gloves
- After contact with body fluids
- Before handling food or medication
- After using the toilet

**Personal Protective Equipment**

Appropriate PPE must be worn when there is a risk of exposure to body fluids:

- Disposable gloves
- Disposable aprons
- Face masks (when required)
- Eye protection (when required)

PPE must be changed between service users and disposed of safely.

### Environmental Cleaning

- High-touch surfaces should be cleaned regularly
- Spills of body fluids must be cleaned immediately using appropriate products
- Cleaning equipment should be colour-coded for different areas
- Single-use cloths are preferred

### Waste Management

- Clinical waste must be disposed of in yellow bags
- Sharps must be disposed of in approved sharps containers
- Domestic waste should be disposed of separately

### Outbreak Management

In the event of an outbreak:

a) Isolate affected individuals where possible

b) Increase cleaning frequency

c) Notify the local Health Protection Team

d) Restrict movement between areas

e) Keep detailed records of cases

### Staff Health

Staff should not work if they have symptoms of infectious illness. Any concerns about occupational exposure should be reported immediately.
