**POLICY STATEMENT**

Acme Care Services recognises the importance of maintaining confidentiality in all aspects of our work. This policy sets out our commitment to protecting confidential information and provides guidance on handling sensitive data appropriately.

**SCOPE**

This policy applies to all information relating to:

- Service users and their families
- Staff members
- Business operations and commercial matters
- Third parties with whom we work

### Principles of Confidentiality

a) Information should only be shared on a need-to-know basis

b) Consent should be obtained before sharing personal information, except where there is a legal duty or overriding public interest

c) Information should be stored securely and accessed only by authorised personnel

d) Discussions about service users should take place in private settings

e) Care should be taken when using electronic communications

### When Confidentiality May Be Breached

Confidentiality may be breached without consent in the following circumstances:

- To prevent serious harm to the individual or others
- Where there is a court order requiring disclosure
- Where there is a statutory duty to report (e.g., safeguarding concerns)
- To prevent or detect serious crime

In such cases, only the minimum necessary information should be shared with appropriate authorities.

### Staff Responsibilities

All staff must:

- Sign a confidentiality agreement as part of their employment
- Not discuss service users in public places
- Secure records and devices when not in use
- Report any breaches of confidentiality immediately
- Dispose of confidential waste securely

### Record Keeping

Records containing confidential information will be:

- Kept accurate and up to date
- Stored securely with appropriate access controls
- Retained only for as long as necessary
- Disposed of securely when no longer required

### Breach of Confidentiality

Breaches of confidentiality will be treated seriously and may result in disciplinary action, up to and including dismissal. Serious breaches may also be reported to relevant regulatory bodies.
