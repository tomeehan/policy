**POLICY STATEMENT**

Acme Care Services is committed to the safe handling, administration, and management of medication. This policy ensures that all medication is handled in accordance with current legislation, professional guidance, and best practice.

**AIM OF THE POLICY**

To ensure that:

- Medication is administered safely and accurately
- Service users receive their medication as prescribed
- Staff are trained and competent to handle medication
- Records are maintained accurately
- Errors are identified, reported and learned from

### Levels of Support

We provide the following levels of medication support:

**Level 1 - Prompting**
Reminding service users to take their medication. The service user remains responsible for their own medication.

**Level 2 - Assisting**
Helping service users to take medication from original packaging. This may include opening containers.

**Level 3 - Administering**
Taking full responsibility for giving medication, typically from a monitored dosage system.

### Staff Training

Staff who handle medication must:

a) Complete medication training before administering medication

b) Be assessed as competent by a qualified trainer

c) Receive regular refresher training

d) Never administer medication outside their level of competence

### Administration Procedures

When administering medication, staff must:

- Check the service user's identity
- Check the medication against the Medication Administration Record (MAR)
- Check the medication has not expired
- Administer according to the prescribed route and dosage
- Record administration immediately on the MAR
- Never sign for medication in advance

### Storage

Medication must be:

- Stored at the correct temperature
- Kept in a secure location
- Kept in original packaging
- Checked regularly for expiry dates

### Controlled Drugs

Additional procedures apply to controlled drugs, including dual signatures and separate record keeping.

### Errors and Incidents

All medication errors must be reported immediately. Errors will be investigated, and appropriate action taken to prevent recurrence.
